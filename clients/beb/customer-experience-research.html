2. Insider Tips from Reviews

What to Bring

Compiled from TripAdvisor, Yelp, Google, blog posts, and the BEB website:

  • Food & drinks — BYOB is allowed! Bring a picnic, snacks, charcuterie, sandwiches, etc.
  • Your favorite playlist — Boats have Bluetooth speakers. Curate a playlist in advance.
  • Sunscreen — Open deck areas get direct sun.
  • Sunglasses — Glare off the water is real.
  • An extra layer of clothing — It's always cooler on the water, even in summer. Reviewers specifically call this out. One noted being comfortable on a 60-degree day with 18 mph winds thanks to the window enclosures.
  • Non-glass containers — NO GLASS ALLOWED. Bring cans, plastic cups, or aluminum bottles. A cleanup fee is charged if glass breaks.
  • Camera / phone — The skyline views, USS Constitution, bridges, and harbor scenery are photo-worthy.

What NOT to Bring

  • No glass bottles or glassware of any kind (cleanup fee if glass breaks).
  • No hard liquor — Beer, wine, and seltzers ONLY. No straight spirits.
  • No excessive amounts of alcohol — BEB reserves the right to restrict customers who bring too much.

Reviewer Tips & Advice

1. "It's like a large golf cart on water" — Multiple reviewers describe the 6 mph max speed this way. Don't expect a speedboat; embrace the leisurely pace.

2. The GPS route is your friend — BEB provides a pre-set suggested route on a digital screen. Follow it for the best sightseeing loop.

3. The 30-minute acclimation period is real — First-timers report feeling fully comfortable after about 30 minutes of driving. The staff walkthrough before departure is thorough.

4. Window enclosures are a game-changer — Clear plastic side windows can be opened for breeze on warm days or closed for wind/rain protection. Multiple reviewers praise this feature for cooler days.

5. Book well in advance for weekends and sunset slots — Limited availability during peak season. Weekend sunset slots go fast.

6. Let BEB provision you — One TripAdvisor reviewer ordered charcuterie and dessert trays through BEB and said they were "excellent." The Sweet Lizzy P dessert boxes are a popular add-on.

7. Arrive 15 minutes early — Gives time for parking, check-in, and the orientation briefing.

8. The captain doesn't drink — Whoever is driving should abstain from alcohol. Passengers can drink freely.

9. You don't need boating experience — Literally every review mentions how easy it is. A driver's license is the only "qualification."

10. Fall is underrated — Reviewers call out fall as having clear skies, brilliant harbor colors, and peaceful boating with fewer crowds.

What You'll See on the Harbor

  • USS Constitution ("Old Ironsides") — you can get close
  • Tobin Bridge
  • Zakim Bridge
  • Boston skyline panorama
  • Boston Tea Party ships
  • Charlestown Navy Yard
  • Harbor islands in the distance
  • Various sailboats and harbor traffic

5. Tipping Information

Does BEB Mention Tipping?

No specific tipping policy was found on BEB's website, FAQ, or in any customer reviews. This is a gap in their current customer communications.

Self-Drive Rentals ("Be Your Own Captain")

  • There is no captain to tip — you are driving the boat yourself.
  • Dock staff who help with orientation, safety briefing, and launch: A $10–$20 tip per group is appreciated but not expected or required. This is consistent with general marina tipping etiquette.
  • Tipping is more of a "nice gesture" for self-drive; it is not an industry expectation the way it is for captained charters.

Captain Provided Charters ($639+)

  • Industry standard: 10–20% of the charter fee, with 15% being the norm.
  • For a $639 charter: $64–$128 tip range (15% = ~$96).
  • For a $659 weekend charter: $66–$132 tip range (15% = ~$99).
  • Cash is preferred and given directly to the captain at the end of the charter.
  • Exceptional service merits a higher tip (20%+).

General Guidance for Chatbot

Suggested chatbot response: "Tipping is not required but always appreciated. For our Captain Provided Charters, a gratuity of 15–20% is customary and can be given directly to your captain in cash at the end of the trip. For self-drive rentals, our dock staff appreciate tips but they are entirely optional."

Weather Policy (for chatbot context)

  • Boats operate in rain and cooler weather (canopy + window enclosures)
  • BEB cancels only for dangerous conditions: high winds, heavy rain storms, severe lightning, rough seas
  • Weather cancellations get a rain check or refund (minus 10% processing fee)
  • Peek Protect plan available at checkout for full refund flexibility (24hr notice)

  • Additional parking garage and lots within walking distance
